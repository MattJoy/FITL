Fire in the Lake is a 1–4 player COIN wargame set in Vietnam, 1964–1972. Four factions — US, ARVN, NVA, and VC — each pursue distinct military, political, and economic strategies via a shared card deck. This reference summarises all key rules and provides faction-specific cheat sheets for fast play.

Quick Ref

Cheat Sheet — Key Rules at a Glance

Eligibility

  • Execute Op/Event last card → Ineligible next card
  • Did NOT execute → Eligible
  • All Factions start Eligible; reset after each Coup Round
  • Pass → stay Eligible; Insurgent +1 Res, COIN +3 ARVN Res

Card Play Options

  • 1st Eligible: Op (±SA), or Event
  • 2nd Eligible after Op Only: Limited Op (no SA)
  • 2nd Eligible after Op+SA: LimOp OR Event
  • 2nd Eligible after Event: Op (±SA)

Limited Operation

  • Op in 1 space only, no Special Activity
  • Patrol/Sweep/March: pieces from multiple spaces but 1 destination
  • Non-Players always get full Op+SA instead of LimOp

Resources

  • Max 75; tracked on edge track
  • US spends ARVN Resources (above Econ marker only)
  • NVA/VC may transfer up to half Resources to each other during their turn
  • Insurgents earn +1 per Rally (Pass only)

Support & Opposition

  • 5 levels: Active Support → Passive Support → Neutral → Passive Opposition → Active Opposition
  • Active counts double Population for totals
  • Pop 0 spaces and LoCs always Neutral

Control

  • COIN Control: US+ARVN pieces together exceed NVA+VC combined
  • NVA Control: NVA pieces alone exceed ALL others (including VC)
  • Adjust markers immediately when pieces change

Stacking & Placement

  • Max 2 Bases per Province/City (any mix of Factions)
  • Bases may NOT be on LoCs
  • Only NVA/VC may be in North Vietnam
  • New Guerrillas/SF always placed Underground

Underground vs Active

  • Guerrillas/SF start Underground (symbol-end down)
  • Assault/Air Strike cannot remove Underground Guerrillas
  • Sweep and Patrol Activate Guerrillas (flip up)
  • Coup Reset flips ALL Guerrillas/SF back Underground

Tunneled Bases

  • When base would be removed: roll a die
  • 1–3: Nothing happens, stop removing in that space
  • 4–6: Remove Tunnel marker only (Base stays)
  • NVA can Infiltrate to flip a VC Tunnel to NVA

Assault Removal Priority

  • 1. NVA Troops first
  • 2. Active NVA or VC Guerrillas (attacker chooses order)
  • 3. Bases — only once no Guerrillas remain
  • Underground Guerrillas block Base removal

Monsoon

  • Active when a Coup card is showing as next card
  • No Sweep, no March
  • Air Strike and Air Lift limited to 2 spaces
  • No Pivotal Events played during Monsoon

Events

  • Event text overrides rules (except stacking/North Vietnam)
  • Dual-Use: choose unshaded or shaded text
  • Capabilities: lasting for rest of game
  • Momentum: lasting until next Coup Reset
  • Pivotal Events: cancel current card, preempt Eligibility order

Coup Round Sequence

  • 1. Victory Phase (check win)
  • 2. Resources Phase (Sabotage → Econ → Earnings)
  • 3. Support Phase (Pacification / Agitation)
  • 4. Redeploy Phase
  • 5. Commitment Phase (US forces in/out)
  • 6. Reset Phase (all Guerrillas Underground, Eligible)

Trail Track (0–4)

  • Raised by: NVA Rally (+2 Res), Events
  • Lowered by: Air Strike (2 hits), COIN controlling Laos/Cambodia (Coup), Events, Reset (if at 0→1 or 4→3)
  • Affects NVA Rally, March speed, Infiltrate, and NVA Earnings

Free Operations

  • No Resource cost; do NOT affect Eligibility
  • EXCEPTION: Pacification, Agitation, Trail Improvement still cost Resources
  • 2nd Eligible gets Event option if 1st used a Special Activity
§ 2

Sequence of Play

2.2 Card Play Flow

1

Reveal top card from draw deck → place on played pile. Reveal next card for preview (players always see 1 card ahead).

2

If Event Card: determine Faction order from symbols left-to-right on card. Skip Ineligible Factions.

3

1st Eligible: Pass, play the Event, or execute an Operation (with or without a Special Activity).

4

2nd Eligible: options depend on what 1st Eligible did (see Cheat Sheet above).

5

Adjust Eligibility cylinders. Executed Op or Event → Ineligible. Did not execute → Eligible.

6

If Coup Card: resolve immediate card effect, then conduct a full Coup Round (§ 6).

Record keeping: Place a cylinder of the executing Faction's color in the matching Sequence of Play box to track actions.

2.3.8 Pivotal Events

Each Faction (Medium/Full scenarios) holds 1 Pivotal Event. Play it to cancel the current card if:

  • That Faction is Eligible
  • Red pre-condition on card is met
  • 1st Eligible has not yet acted
  • No Coup is showing as next card

Trumping order: VC > ARVN > NVA > US (US cannot trump any).

2.4.1 RVN Leader

Most recently played Coup card is the current RVN Leader. Its text is in effect as a lingering Event.

"Failed Attempt" cards only cancel "Duong Van Minh" and do not count as a card in the box for Pivotal Event preconditions.

Operations

Key Rule: An Operation must be executed in at least 1 space to count (or for NVA Rally, Improve the Trail). Once targeted, enemy pieces are affected to the maximum extent possible.

Timing: A Faction may execute its Special Activity at any point immediately before, during, or after its Operation. A Special Activity may only accompany a full Operation (not a Limited Op). No extra Resource cost.

Ambush (VC) VC Same as NVA Ambush but using VC Guerrillas. Same as NVA Ambush rules (see above).
§ 6

Coup Rounds

6.1

Victory Phase: Check if any Faction meets its victory condition. If yes, game ends (see Victory). Otherwise continue.

6.2

Resources Phase: (a) Sabotage unsabotaged LoCs where Insurgent Guerrillas outnumber COIN or adjacent to City without COIN Control. (b) Degrade Trail 1 if any Laos/Cambodia space is COIN-Controlled. (c) ARVN earns Aid + unSabotaged Econ total. (d) Insurgents earn: VC = VC Bases on map; NVA = Laos/Cambodia Bases + (2×Trail). (e) Subtract 3×Casualties from Aid.

6.3

Support Phase: US then ARVN Pacify (combined max 4 spaces): each space must have COIN Control + Police + Faction's Troops; 3 ARVN Res removes Terror or shifts 1 level toward Active Support (max 2 shifts per space per Phase). VC Agitate in up to 4 spaces with VC pieces and no COIN Control; 1 VC Res removes Terror or shifts 1 level toward Active Opposition.

6.4

Redeploy Phase: Remove all US/ARVN from Laos/Cambodia (US Troops out-of-play). ARVN must move Troops from LoCs and Provinces without COIN Bases to Cities/Bases/Saigon; may move Police to LoCs or COIN-Controlled spaces. NVA may move NVA Troops to any NVA Bases. Adjust Control markers.

6.5

Commitment Phase (2nd Ed): Take 1-in-3 US Troop Casualties and all Base Casualties out of play. Return Irregular Casualties to Available. US places remaining Troop Casualties in COIN-Controlled spaces/LoCs/Saigon. US then moves up to (10 minus Casualties placed) US Troops + up to 2 Bases among Available, COIN spaces, LoCs, Saigon. For every 2 US pieces moved map→Available, VC may shift 1 space 1 level toward Opposition.

6.6

Reset Phase: Trail: if 0 → raise to 1; if 4 → lower to 3. Remove ALL Terror and Sabotage markers. Flip ALL Guerrillas and SF Underground. Discard Momentum Events. Mark all Factions Eligible. Play next card.

§ 7

Victory Conditions

During Coup Rounds (§ 7.2)

At the start of each Coup Round, check victory markers against edge track thresholds:

FactionCondition to Win
🟢 USTotal Support + Available US Troops & Bases > 50
🔴 NVANVA-Controlled Population + NVA Bases on map > 18
🟡 ARVNCOIN-Controlled Population + Patronage > 50
🔵 VCTotal Opposition + VC Bases on map > 35

Final Coup Margin (§ 7.3)

If no one wins by final Coup Round, highest margin (amount above or below threshold) wins.

FactionMargin Formula
🟢 USTotal Support + Available US − 50
🔴 NVANVA Pop Controlled + NVA Bases − 18
🟡 ARVNCOIN Pop Controlled + Patronage − 50
🔵 VCTotal Opposition + VC Bases − 35

Tie-break order: Non-Players → VC → ARVN → NVA

Factions

Faction-Specific Rules

US

United States

Counterinsurgent · Olive Cubes

Operations

  • Train: Place 1–2 Irregulars, or at US Bases 1–2 Rangers or up to 6 ARVN cubes per space. US gets Troops in/out via Commitment Phase, not Operations.
  • Patrol: Free (0 Resources). Move cubes through LoCs/Cities; Activate enemies on LoCs.
  • Sweep: Free. Activate 1 enemy Guerrilla per cube/SF (½ in Jungle). Not in Monsoon.
  • Assault: Costs 3 ARVN Res only if adding ARVN follow-up. Count US Troops only. 2 removes per Troop with US Base; 1 remove per Troop normally; ½ per Troop in Highland with no Base.

Special Activities

  • Advise: Sweep/Assault as if ARVN in 1–2 spaces not used for Training. Activate Irregular/Ranger to remove 2 enemies. +6 Aid option.
  • Air Lift: Move any US Troops + up to 4 ARVN pieces among 4 spaces (2 in Monsoon).
  • Air Strike: Roll die = hits. 1 hit = remove 1 Active enemy OR 2 hits = Degrade Trail 1 box (max 1 box). Removes Active pieces; shifts each hit space toward Active Opposition.

Special Rules

  • US does NOT track its own Resources — spends ARVN Resources (only above Total Econ marker)
  • US Troops become Casualties when removed by Attack (not to Available)
  • US forces enter/leave via Commitment Phase; cannot place/move own Troops via Operations
  • US-led Irregulars are Special Forces (SF) — track separately in US-led Irregulars box
  • In Saigon during Train: may transfer up to 3 Patronage → ARVN Resources
🎯 Victory: Total Support + Available US (Troops & Bases) > 50
RVN

ARVN

Counterinsurgent · Yellow Troops, Orange Police

Operations

  • Train: 3 Res if placing pieces. Place 1–2 Rangers or up to 6 ARVN cubes at Cities or US/ARVN Bases. In 1 space, Pacify (needs ARVN Troops + Police + COIN Control) or replace 3 cubes with Base.
  • Patrol: 3 Res total. Move cubes through LoCs/Cities. Police count in Cities and LoCs for Assault; only Troops count in Provinces.
  • Sweep: 3 Res/space. Count cubes + Rangers (Special Forces). In Jungle: 1 Activate per 2 cubes/SF.
  • Assault: 3 Res/space. 1 enemy per 2 cubes (3 in Highland). Police count in Cities/LoCs; Troops only in Provinces.

Special Activities

  • Govern: With Train or Patrol. In COIN-Controlled Provinces/Cities (not Saigon, not Training space): add 3×Pop to Aid OR transfer Pop from Aid→Patronage + shift 1 toward Neutral.
  • Transport: Move up to 6 ARVN Troops/Rangers along LoCs and into destinations. Flips ALL Rangers Underground after.
  • Raid: With Patrol/Sweep/Assault. Move in adjacent Rangers. Activate Underground Ranger to remove 2 enemies (Bases last; Tunneled Bases immune).

Special Rules

  • ARVN Resources are shared — US can spend them above Total Econ, ARVN can spend at or below Econ
  • Aid is added to ARVN Resources each Coup Round; Patronage contributes to victory
  • ARVN Troops must Redeploy from LoCs/Provinces without COIN Bases during Coup Round
  • Rangers begin each Coup Reset as Underground (Transport also resets all Rangers Underground)
  • Patronage: tracks Saigon regime's "corruption" pool (0–75). Lost via Subvert or Govern.
🎯 Victory: COIN-Controlled Population + Patronage > 50
NVA

NVA

Insurgent · Red Cubes

Operations

  • Rally: 1 Res/space (not Support spaces). With NVA Base: place Guerrillas up to Trail + NVA Bases. Can spend +2 Res to Improve Trail (even on LimOp or 0 spaces).
  • March: 1 Res/Province or City entered. Use Trail to extend through Laos/Cambodia. If Trail = 4, Laos/Cambodia spaces cost 0. Not in Monsoon.
  • Attack: 1 Res/space. Roll ≤ Guerrilla count → remove 2 enemies. OR remove 1 enemy per 2 NVA Troops (no roll needed). US removed → Casualties + NVA Attrition.
  • Terror: Activate 1 Underground Guerrilla or NVA Troops can activate instead. In Province/City: place Terror, shift Support toward Neutral. On LoC: place Sabotage.

Special Activities

  • Infiltrate: With Rally or March. At NVA Base: place Troops up to Trail+NVA Bases; swap Guerrillas for Troops. If NVA>VC: shift Opposition toward Neutral + replace 1 VC piece with NVA.
  • Bombard: Needs ≥3 COIN Troops or COIN Base+Troop in space, plus ≥3 NVA Troops in/adjacent. Removes 1 US or ARVN Troop cube (US → Casualties).
  • Ambush: With March or Attack. Underground NVA Guerrilla required. Auto-remove 1 enemy (no roll); activate only 1 Guerrilla; no attrition for US kills. From LoC: remove from any adjacent space.

Special Rules

  • NVA may occupy North Vietnam, Laos, Cambodia — US/ARVN may not stay in foreign spaces after Coup Redeploy
  • Trail (0–4) is the NVA's most important resource: affects Rally, March, Infiltrate, Earnings
  • NVA Earnings: Laos/Cambodia Bases + (2 × Trail value) per Coup Round
  • NVA Control is exclusive — NVA alone must outnumber ALL other factions (including VC)
  • NVA can Infiltrate to convert VC pieces (even Tunneled Bases) to NVA
🎯 Victory: NVA-Controlled Population + NVA Bases on map > 18
VC

Viet Cong

Insurgent · Blue Guerrillas

Operations

  • Rally: 1 Res/space (not Support spaces). With VC Base: place Guerrillas up to Pop + VC Bases, OR flip all Guerrillas Underground. Agitate with "Cadres" Capability (costs Resources even if free Rally).
  • March: 1 Res/Province or City. LoCs free. Moving group Activates if destination is LoC or Support AND combined pieces exceed 3. Not in Monsoon.
  • Attack: 1 Res/space. Roll ≤ Guerrilla count → remove 2 enemies. US pieces → Casualties + Attrition.
  • Terror: Underground VC Guerrilla required. Place Terror marker, shift 1 level toward Active Opposition (stronger than NVA Terror, which only neutralises Support).

Special Activities

  • Tax: Up to 4 spaces with Underground VC Guerrilla, no COIN Control. Gain Econ value (LoC) or 2×Pop Resources. Province/City: shift 1 toward Active Support (activating VC reveals them).
  • Subvert: With Rally, March, or Terror. Remove 2 ARVN cubes or replace 1 with VC Guerrilla; drop Patronage –1 per 2 ARVN removed (round down).
  • Ambush: Same as NVA Ambush. Underground VC Guerrilla required. Auto-remove 1 enemy, no attrition. From LoC: remove from adjacent space.

Special Rules

  • VC Guerrillas are the heart of the VC — manage Underground status carefully
  • VC Earnings: 1 Resource per VC Base on the map each Coup Round
  • VC Agitate during Coup (Support Phase): 1 Res removes Terror or shifts 1 level toward Active Opposition
  • VC Terror shifts toward Active Opposition (NVA only removes Support — both useful for different victory goals)
  • VC can Subvert to erode ARVN power and Patronage; key for disrupting ARVN victory
  • VC LoC Taxation: ignores COIN Control on LoCs — powerful resource tool even in enemy territory
🎯 Victory: Total Opposition + VC Bases on map > 35
§ 8

Non-Player Factions (Solo / 2–3 Players)

Non-Players always get full Op + Special Activity instead of Limited Operations. They execute Events only when they would have an actual effect. They follow priority flowcharts, not player preference.

General Non-Player Rules

Piece Priority (placing): Bases first, then Guerrillas/SF, then alternating Troops and Police (fewest in space first; Troops if tied). Target enemy Underground before Active Guerrillas, US before ARVN, VC before NVA.

Piece Priority (removing friendly): Reverse of above — Active before Underground Guerrillas; most in space first.

Space selection: Follow the priority flowchart in order to maximum extent, then randomly via the Random Spaces Map (roll two dice: tens-die then ones-die → space number, follow arrows until candidate reached).

LoC Priority (if LoCs and other spaces are equal): Choose LoCs last. Among LoCs: highest Econ first, then adjacent to random City, then equal chance die roll.

Dual-Use Events: NVA/VC always use shaded text; US/ARVN always use unshaded text (unless special Event instructions say otherwise).

Pivotal Events: Non-players play their Pivotal Event the instant it is playable AND any player Faction is 1st Eligible.

Non-Player VC Priority (§ 8.5)

  • Terror: If enough Resources + Underground Guerrillas to Terror any Support space, or to shift Pop ≥ 2d6 toward Active Opposition → Terror. Then Tax or Subvert.
  • Event/Pass: Check Capability cards next/current. Roll vs Coup cards in RVN Leader box to decide Pass or Event.
  • Rally: If would place ≥2 VC Bases, or 3d6 < Available Guerrillas → Rally. Then Tax or Subvert.
  • March: Get Guerrillas onto LoCs, then into Pop spaces at Neutral/Support, finally remove NVA Control. Then Ambush, Tax, or Subvert.
  • Attack: If ≥1 Resource; Ambush first, then Attack with ≥3 Active Guerrillas. Then Tax if no Ambush.
  • Pass if nothing else possible.

Non-Player NVA Priority (§ 8.6)

  • Attack: If Ambush/Troop Attacks can remove a COIN Base or ≥1d6 enemy pieces → Attack. Ambush first, then Troop Attack, then Guerrilla Attack.
  • Event/Pass: Same Capability roll logic as VC. Pass if 0 Resources.
  • Terror: If can Terrorize Support spaces with total Pop ≥ 1d6 → do so. Then Bombard.
  • Rally: If Trail ≤1, NVA Base has <2 units, 2d6 > Available Guerrillas, or would place Base → Rally. Then Improve Trail. Then Infiltrate.
  • March: Set up Laos/Cambodia Base, get Guerrillas on LoCs, expand NVA Control, mass Troops adjacent to COIN Control. Then Ambush (only if removes a Base), then Infiltrate or Bombard.
  • Pass if nothing else possible.

Non-Player ARVN Priority (§ 8.7)

  • Event/Pass: Check Capability cards; roll vs Coup cards in RVN Leader box. Pass if would bring Resources to 0.
  • Assault: If can remove a Base, remove NVA Control, or remove ≥3 enemies → Assault. Then Govern or Raid.
  • Train: If would place ≥2 Bases or remove COIN Control → Train. Then Govern.
  • Patrol: If can Activate ≥1d6 Guerrillas → Patrol. Then Govern or Raid.
  • Sweep: If can Activate ≥1d6 Guerrillas → Sweep. Then Raid.
  • Pass.

Non-Player US Priority (§ 8.8)

  • Event/Pass: Same Capability card check. Pass if would reduce Available below 15 Troops + Bases.
  • Air Strike: If can remove a Base, Degrade Trail, or remove ≥1d6 Active enemies → Air Strike. Add Aid if ≥1 Base removed.
  • Assault: If can remove a Base, remove NVA Control, or remove ≥6 enemies → Assault. Then Advise.
  • Train: If would Pacify ≥1 space, place ≥2 Bases, or remove COIN Control → Train. Then Advise.
  • Sweep/Patrol: If can Activate ≥1d6 Guerrillas → Sweep or Patrol. Then Advise or Air Lift.
  • Pass.
